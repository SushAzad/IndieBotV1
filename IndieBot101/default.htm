Grameen Saathi

Problem

  1. If we need information about anything, we just unlock our phones and search for it on the internet.
  2. But what about our rural compatriots? Access to internet and technology in India is yet to percolate to rural India.
  3. Wikipedia shows that 87% of households in India have a phone connection. However, internet penetration in rural India is only 15.7%.
  4. Take our farmers for eg.
    • Current Scenario:
      1. They have access only to passive mediums of information:
        1. TV, Radio, Newspaper
      2. This only shows them whatever info is supplied for mass consumption.
    •  Result:
      1. There is nowhere they can search for information specific to their needs.
    •  Without access to competitive price from many dealers, they will usually have to depend on one middleman to sell whichever crops they have harvested.
    • With no central database to search the item they need, they can’t buy from the best seller. Purchasing options are limited to shops that they know.
    • Even getting accurate weather forecast to harvest crops accordingly is tough without access to information.
  5. Until technology penetration catches up, how can we bridge the gap using the technology available today?

 

How we are solving it

Introducing Grameen Saathi!

  1. Grameen Saathi is an intelligent bot that can converse with a phone caller in their native language.
  2. Callers can request for information about anything, eg: where to buy/sell a product, get weather forecast, etc.
  3. Grameen Saathi then translates the query into English, runs a localized search, parses the result, and returns it to the caller in their native language.

 

What it does (Current Hackathon Build)

Grameen Saathi is built on the Microsoft Bot Builder SDK, and published on Azure Cloud Services.

Currently, it is capable of:

  1. Accepting both text and speech input.
  2. Finding purchasers/dealers for any product that the farmer may want to sell (Eg. Jowar, Rice, etc.)
  3. Finding sellers in the area who sell the product the farmer may want to buy (Eg. Organic Fertilizer, Seeds, etc.)
  4. Providing a 10-day weather forecast in the region.

 

How we can extend it:

  1. Native language support.
  2. Once Indian languages are included in the Microsoft Translator API, we will use that to provide the translation from native language to English for search and processing and convert response to native language.
  3. We can add additional features in the future, like:
    • Getting info about which crops are in demand in the market.
    • How much fertilizer to use for each different grain, etc. 

The possibilities are endless :) 

 

Benefits:

  1. Eliminate the middle-man and the related commissions that go out of the farmer’s pocket.
  2. No need for ‘contacts’ to buy/sell goods and products.
  3. Guarantee of best price because farmer now has access to open market and competitive prices for his produce.
  4. In flood, when minimal connectivity (TV lines are down / newspaper not delivered etc), they can call Gramin Saathi for assistance with the nearest emergency centre.

 

Grameen Saathi adds a new dimension in our quest to increase access to internet in rural India, by a simple phone call.

To run Grameen Saathi, just download the botframework-emulator, and set the endpoint to this url and /api/messages, like: 

https://your_bots_hostname/api/messages
